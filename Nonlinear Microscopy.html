Pulse-shaping Fluorescence Microscopy

As its name suggests, a broadband femtosecond laser such as a mode-locked Ti:Sapphire laser emits a large number of wavelengths. In a nonlinear interaction such as two-photon microscopy, two photons from the pulsed laser beam are absorbed to bridge a resonance such that the combined photon energies match the energy of the two-photon transition.
